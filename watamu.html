Discover Watamu

Watamu, located on Kenya’s north coast about 600 km from Nairobi, is a marine paradise with a UNESCO-listed marine park, ancient ruins, and turtle conservation projects. Its golden beaches, coral reefs, and Arabuko Sokoke Forest offer a mix of adventure and relaxation.

From snorkeling with colorful fish to exploring Gede Ruins or participating in turtle releases, Watamu is ideal for marine lovers and cultural explorers.

Frequently Asked Questions

What’s the best time to visit Watamu?

June to October offers calm seas for diving and turtle nesting season. November to March is warmer but great for marine activities.

What marine activities are available in Watamu?

Watamu offers snorkeling, scuba diving, dolphin watching, and turtle conservation tours in the marine park.

What should I pack for Watamu?

Pack swimwear, sunscreen, a hat, snorkeling gear, comfortable shoes for ruins, and modest attire for cultural respect.

Is Watamu safe for swimming?

Yes, Watamu’s beaches are safe for swimming, with protected reefs. Always follow local guides’ advice for marine activities.

Can I combine Watamu with other destinations?

Yes, Watamu pairs well with Malindi for coastal exploration or Tsavo for a safari-beach combo.

How long should I stay in Watamu?

A 3-5 day stay allows time for marine activities, ruins exploration, and beach relaxation.
