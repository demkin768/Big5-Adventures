Discover Malindi

Nestled on the beautiful Kenyan coast, Malindi is a tropical paradise that offers the perfect blend of relaxation, adventure, and cultural immersion. With its pristine beaches, rich Swahili history, and vibrant coral reefs, Malindi has been a favorite destination for travelers for centuries.

Founded around the 1st century AD, Malindi's history includes Swahili settlement, Arab influence, and Portuguese exploration. Vasco da Gama visited in 1498 and erected a coral pillar that still stands today. This fascinating history is visible in the town's architecture, cuisine, and cultural sites like the ancient Gedi Ruins nearby.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best time to visit Malindi?

Malindi is a year-round destination, but the best weather is during the dry seasons from January to March and July to October. These periods offer sunny days with minimal rainfall, perfect for beach activities and water sports. The rainy seasons (April-June and November) still have plenty of sunshine but may experience short tropical showers.

Do I need a visa to visit Kenya?

Most visitors to Kenya require a visa, which can be obtained online through the eVisa portal before travel or upon arrival at the airport. It's recommended to check the latest visa requirements based on your nationality well in advance of your trip. The eVisa process is straightforward and typically processed within a few business days.

Is Malindi safe for tourists?

Yes, Malindi is generally safe for tourists. Like any tourist destination, it's important to take standard precautions: don't leave valuables unattended on the beach, use hotel safes for passports and extra cash, and avoid walking alone in isolated areas at night. Your hotel can advise on any areas to avoid and arrange reliable transportation.

What should I pack for a beach holiday in Malindi?

Essentials include: light cotton clothing, swimwear, sun protection (hat, sunscreen, sunglasses), insect repellent, comfortable walking shoes for exploring historical sites, a light jacket for cooler evenings, and any necessary medications. Many restaurants and hotels have a relaxed dress code, but it's respectful to cover up when away from the beach.

What currency is used in Malindi?

The Kenyan Shilling (KES) is the local currency. US Dollars and Euros are widely accepted in tourist areas, but you'll get better value paying in local currency. ATMs are available in Malindi town, and credit cards are accepted at most hotels and larger restaurants. It's advisable to carry some cash for smaller establishments and markets.

What languages are spoken in Malindi?

Swahili is the national language, but English is widely spoken in tourist areas, hotels, and restaurants. The local community also speaks Giriama, a Mijikenda language. Learning a few basic Swahili phrases like "Jambo" (Hello), "Asante" (Thank you), and "Habari" (How are you?) will be appreciated by locals.
