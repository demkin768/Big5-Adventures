Discover Lake Turkana

Known as the Jade Sea, Lake Turkana is the world’s largest permanent desert lake, located in Kenya’s remote northern frontier, about 700 km from Nairobi. Its striking turquoise waters, fossil-rich shores, and vibrant Turkana and El Molo cultures make it a unique destination for adventurers and history enthusiasts.

From exploring UNESCO-listed fossil sites at Koobi Fora to boat tours spotting Nile crocodiles or engaging with local communities, Lake Turkana offers a blend of natural beauty, cultural richness, and archaeological wonder.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Lake Turkana safe to visit?

Yes, with guided tours and experienced operators, Lake Turkana is safe. The remote location requires proper planning, included in our packages.

What’s the best time to visit Lake Turkana?

The dry season (June-October) is ideal for comfortable travel and clear lake views. Avoid the rainy season due to challenging road conditions.

What should I pack for Lake Turkana?

Pack lightweight clothing, sunscreen, a hat, sturdy shoes, binoculars for wildlife, and a jacket for cooler nights in the desert.

Are cultural tours respectful to local communities?

Yes, our tours are designed with respect for Turkana and El Molo communities, supporting local guides and sustainable practices.

How do I get to Lake Turkana?

Our packages include transport from Nairobi via 4x4 vans or cruisers, suitable for the remote roads. Flights to Lodwar are available for luxury packages.

How long should I stay at Lake Turkana?

A 3-4 day trip is ideal to explore fossil sites, enjoy boat tours, and engage with local cultures, given the remote location.
