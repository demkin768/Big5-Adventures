Discover Lake Naivasha

Lake Naivasha, nestled in Kenya’s Great Rift Valley just 90 km from Nairobi, is a freshwater lake renowned for its abundant wildlife and serene beauty. Home to hippos, over 400 bird species, and the unique Crescent Island, it offers boat safaris, walking tours, and birdwatching opportunities.

Whether you’re exploring the lake by boat, strolling among grazing animals, or relaxing at a lakeside lodge, Lake Naivasha is a perfect blend of adventure and tranquility.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it safe to do boat safaris on Lake Naivasha?

Yes, boat safaris are safe with experienced guides who ensure safe distances from hippos and provide life jackets.

What’s the best time to visit Lake Naivasha?

The dry seasons (June-October, January-February) are ideal for boat trips and walking safaris, with clear weather and vibrant wildlife.

What should I pack for Lake Naivasha?

Pack light clothing, sunscreen, a hat, binoculars for birdwatching, comfortable walking shoes, and a light jacket for evenings.

Can I walk on Crescent Island without a guide?

Guided walks are recommended for safety and to learn about the wildlife, but experienced visitors may explore with prior arrangement.

Can I combine Lake Naivasha with Hell’s Gate?

Yes, Hell’s Gate is just 10 km away, and many packages combine cycling in Hell’s Gate with boat safaris in Naivasha.

How long should I stay at Lake Naivasha?

A 1-2 day visit covers boat safaris, Crescent Island walks, and birdwatching. Add a day for Hell’s Gate or relaxation.
