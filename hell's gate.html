Discover Hell’s Gate

Hell’s Gate National Park, located in Kenya’s Great Rift Valley near Nairobi, is a compact yet spectacular destination known for its dramatic gorges, geothermal activity, and unique cycling safaris. Spanning 68 square kilometers, the park offers a rare opportunity to explore wildlife on foot or by bike, with no predators like lions or leopards.

From scaling Fischer’s Tower to soaking in natural hot springs or hiking Ol Njorowa Gorge, Hell’s Gate is perfect for adventure seekers and nature lovers looking for an active safari experience.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Hell’s Gate safe for cycling?

Yes, Hell’s Gate is safe for cycling as it has no large predators like lions or leopards. Guided tours ensure safety and provide helmets and bikes.

What’s the best time to visit Hell’s Gate?

The dry seasons (June-October, January-February) are ideal for cycling and hiking, with clear trails and less mud. Wet seasons offer lush scenery.

What should I pack for Hell’s Gate?

Pack comfortable cycling/hiking gear, sunscreen, a hat, water bottle, sturdy shoes, and a light jacket for early mornings. Bikes are provided.

Is rock climbing experience required?

No, beginner-friendly routes like Fischer’s Tower are available with expert guides. Experienced climbers can tackle advanced routes.

Can I combine Hell’s Gate with Lake Naivasha?

Yes, Hell’s Gate is close to Lake Naivasha, and many packages include boat trips or visits to Crescent Island for a combined adventure.

How long should I stay in Hell’s Gate?

A 1-2 day visit is ideal for cycling, hiking, and hot spring tours. Add a day for Lake Naivasha or climbing activities.
